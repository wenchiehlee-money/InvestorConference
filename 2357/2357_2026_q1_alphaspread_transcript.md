Asustek Computer Inc
Investor Relations

Asustek Computer, best known as ASUS, designs and sells personal computers, laptops, gaming rigs, motherboards, graphics cards, monitors, routers, and other computer hardware. It is one of the key brands people and businesses turn to when they want a new PC or the parts that go inside one. The company sits close to the end of the electronics value chain, turning chips and components from suppliers into finished products under its own brand. Its customers include everyday consumers, gamers, small businesses, and larger organizations that buy laptops, desktops, networking gear, and IT hardware through retailers, distributors, and direct sales channels. ASUS makes money mainly by selling physical devices and components, with a mix of branded consumer products and parts sold to builders and system integrators. That means its business depends on product design, brand reputation, and keeping up with fast-moving PC and gaming demand. What makes ASUS different is that it is not just a PC maker; it also has a strong position in motherboard and graphics card hardware, which gives it deep roots in the computing ecosystem. That lets the company serve both mainstream buyers and technical users who care about performance, upgradeability, and compatibility. In simple terms, ASUS sells the tools and machines that power personal computing, gaming, and home and office connectivity.
